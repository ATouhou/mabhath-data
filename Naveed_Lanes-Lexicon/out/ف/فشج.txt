فشج1
فَشَجَ, aor.
فَشِجَ, (S, O, K,) inf. n. فَشْجٌ, (A'Obeyd,
TA,) He parted his legs, or made an opening between them, (A 'Obeyd, S, O, K,) but less than is
denoted by تَفَاجَّ, (A 'Obeyd, TA,) previously to
making water; as also ↓ فشّج, (S, O, K,) inf. n.
تَفْشِيجٌ; (S, O;) or the latter signifies he did so
[much, i. e.,] in a greater degree than is denoted
by the former verb: (TA:) [and فَشَحَ and فشّح
signify the same:] and ↓ تفشّج [likewise] is syn.
with نَفَحَّجَ [signifying the same as فَشَجَ]; (Lth,
S, K;) or he did so at the fire. (Lth, L.) And
فَشَجَتْ (T, TA) and ↓ تفشّجت and ↓ انفشجت, (T,
O, TA,) as also انفشحت, with ح, (IAar, O,) are
said of a she-camel, (T, O, TA,) meaning She
parted her [hind] legs widely, to be milked or to
stale. (T, TA.) ―
-b2-  And فَشَجَ عَنْهُ, and ↓ فشّج
He declined, deviated, or turned aside or away,
from him, or it; like فَشَحَ and فشّح. (TA in
art. فشح.)
