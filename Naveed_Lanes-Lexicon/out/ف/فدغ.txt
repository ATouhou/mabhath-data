فدغ1
فَدَغَهُ, aor.
فَدَغَ, inf. n. فَدْغٌ, He broke it, (S, O,
Msb, K,) or crushed it; (S, O, K;) or (K) it is
said when the object is a hollow thing, (S, O,
Msb, K, TA,) or a moist, or soft, thing, (TA,) a
person's head, (S, O, TA,) and a grape, and the
like: (O, TA:) and he bruised, brayed, or pounded,
it coarsely: and he clave, split, or rent, it slightly.
(TA.) ―
-b2-  And فَدَغَ الطَّعَامَ He put much clarified
butter into the food. (O, * K, * TA.) ―
-b3-  And one
says also, فَدَغَ الكَمْأَةَ فِى السَّمْنِ [app. meaning He
preserved the truffles in clarified butter]. (O.)
7
انفدغ It (anything dry, or rigid,) became
soft, or supple. (O, K. *)
فَدَغٌ Distortion in the foot: (Ibn-'Abbád, O,
K:) like فَدَعٌ, which is more common. (O.)
[See فَدَعٌ.]
مِفْدَغٌ An instrument for breaking, or crushing.
(K, TA.) And applied to a man; like مِدَقٌّ [q. v.].
