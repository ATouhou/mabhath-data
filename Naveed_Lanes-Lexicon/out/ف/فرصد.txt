فرصدفِرْصِدٌ and ↓ فِرْصِيْدٌ The stones of raisins and of
grapes; (M, O, L, K;) as also ↓ فِرْصَادٌ. (M,
L, K.)
فِرْصَادٌ The [mulberry called] تُوت [q. v.]: (AO,
AHn, O, Msb:) or the [tree called] تُوت: or its
fruit: (M, K:) or the red توت: (S, Msb:) [or,
accord. to Zeyn el-'Attár, the sweet and white mulberry: so says Golius: see تُوتٌ:] Lth says that
it is a well-known tree; that the people of ElBasrah call the tree thus, and call its fruit تُوتٌ:
(T, O, * Msb:) and by فِرْصَادٌ the lawyers mean
the tree that bears the [fruit called] تُوت. (Msb.)
―
-b2-  Also A red dye; (K:) or redness. (M, L.)
