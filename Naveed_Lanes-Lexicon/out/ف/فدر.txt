فدر1
فَدَرَ, (Lth, IAar, T, S, M, O, K,) aor.
فَدُرَ,
(M,) or
فَدِرَ, (O, K,) inf. n. فُدُورٌ (Lth, T, S, M, O,
K) and فَدْرٌ; (K;) and ↓ فدّر, (IAar, T, O, K,)
inf. n. تَفْدِيرٌ; (O;) and ↓ افدر; (IAar, T, O, K;)
said of a stallion, (IAar, T, S, &c.,) primarily of
a stallion-camel, (IAar, T,) He desisted from
covering; (IAar, T, S, O;) or he desisted from
covering, being wearied by much indulging therein:
(S, O:) or he flagged, or became remiss, or languid, in covering, (Lth, T, M, K,) and desisted
therefrom: (M, K:) the و in فَدَرَ, thus used, may
be a substitute for the ت in فَتَرَ: (O:) accord. to
IAth, it signifies he lacked power, or ability, to
cover. (TA.) ―
