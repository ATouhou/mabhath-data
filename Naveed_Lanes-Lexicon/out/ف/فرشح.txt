فرشحQ. 1
فَرْشَحَ, inf. n. فَرْشَحَةٌ (S, K) and فَرْشَحَى,
(K,) He (a man, S) parted his legs; (K:) or he
parted his legs in sitting; and so فَرْشَطَ: (Lh, S:)
or he spread his legs wide apart: (A 'Obeyd, TA:)
and he stood with his legs wide apart in prayer;
(Ks, S;) and so فرشح رِجْلَيْهِ: (S:) or he sat in
a relaxed state, with his thighs cleaving to the
ground; (K;) and so فَرْشَطَ: (TA:) or he leaped,
or jumped; (K;) or made short leaps  or jumps.
(TA.) And فَرْشَحَتْ, (S, L, and so in some
copies of the K,) or ↓ تَفَرْشَحَتْ, (thus in other
copies of the K, and thus in the CK,) She (a
camel) parted her kind legs to be milked (S, L,
K) and to stale. (L.)
