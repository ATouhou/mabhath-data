فجع1
فَجَعَهُ, (S, MA, O, K,) aor.
فَجَعَ, (O, K,) inf. n.
فَجْعٌ (MA, O) and فَجِيعَةٌ; (MA; [or this is a
simple subst.;]) and ↓ فجّعهُ, inf. n. تَفْجِيعٌ, (S,
K,) or the latter verb has an intensive meaning;
(O, TA;) It pained him; (S, MA, O, K;)
afflicted, or distressed, him; disordered him, or
rendered him diseased: (MA:) one says, فَجَعَتْهُ
المُصِيبَةُ The affliction, calamity, or misfortune,
pained him: (S, O:) or فَجْعٌ [expl. as the inf. n.
of the pass. verb] signifies a man's being pained
by the loss of a thing that is highly esteemed by
him (يَكْرُمُ عَلَيْهِ); (Lth, O, K, TA;) such as property or cattle, and offspring, and a relation, or
person beloved: (TA:) you say, فُجِعَ بِمَالِهِ (Lth,
O, K) and بِوَلَدِهِ (Lth, O) [He was pained by the
loss of his property or cattle, and his offspring]:
and فَجَعْتُهُ فِى مَالِهِ and أَهْلِهِ [I pained, afflicted,
or distressed, him, in, or in respect of, his property
or cattle, and his family], aor.
فَجَعَ, inf. n. فَجْعٌ.
