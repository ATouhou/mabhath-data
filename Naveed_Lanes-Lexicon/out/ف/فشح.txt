man, TK) parted his legs, or made an opening
between them; (K;) like فَشَجَ; both mentioned
by Th, on the authority of IAar; (TA;) as also
↓ فشّح, (K,) inf. n. تَفْشِيحٌ; and likewise with ج,
as mentioned by Th. (TA.) ―
-b2-  And فَشَحَ عَنْهُ,
and ↓ فشّح, He declined, deviated, or turned aside
or away, from him, or it; (K;) and so فَشَجَ and
فشّج. (TA.)
