فأR. Q. 1
فَأْفَأَ, (T, M, Msb,) inf. n. فَأْفَأَةٌ, (T,
S, M, Msb, K,) He reiterated the letter ف (Mbr,
T, S, M, Msb, K) in his speech; (S, M, K;) or
was as though the letter ف predominated upon his
tongue; (T;) or had an impediment in the tongue,
the letter ف predominating in the speech. (M.)
You say, فِيهِ فَأْفَأَةٌ [In him is a fault of reiterating
the letter ف in his speech; &c.] (S, K.) [See also
فَأْفآءٌ.]
فَأْفَأٌ: see what follows.
فَأْفَآءٌ (T, S, M, Msb, K) and ↓ فَأْفَأٌ, (T, M, Msb,
K, [and thus accord. to my copy of the Mgh,])
both mentioned by Lh, (T,) A man in whom is
what is termed فَأْفَأَةٌ, expl. above; (T, S, M, K;)
or who reiterates the letter ف much when he
speaks; (M;) or who cannot utter the word
unless with an effort, commencing with the like of
the letter ف, and then pronouncing with effort the
letters of the word correctly: (Mgh:) or one
who reiterates his words much in speaking:
(TA:) fem. with ة. (T, Msb.).
