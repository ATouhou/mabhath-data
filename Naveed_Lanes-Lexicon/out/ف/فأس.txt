فَأْسٌ A certain implement of iron, (M,) with
which one digs; [i. e., a kind of hoe; thus called
in the present day; generally having a blade
more long than wide, and a short handle;
altogether resembling an adz:] (Kr, M:) and
with which one cuts; [i. e., an adz; and an axe;
both also thus called in the present day; more
commonly the former; used for cutting, cleaving,
and splitting, trees and wood, (see فَأَسَ,) and for
hewing, forming, or fashioning, wood &c.: (see
also قَدُومٌ:)] (M:) a thing well known: (A, K:)
[applied also to a pickaxe: (see صَاقُورٌ:)] the ء in
this word may be suppressed: (Msb:) it is of the
fem. gender: (M, Msb, K:) the pl. (of pauc., O)
is أَفْؤُسٌ (M, O, Msb, K) and (of mult., O) فُؤُوسٌ,
(S, M, O, Msb, K,) and, accord. to some, فُؤْسٌ.
(TA.) ―
