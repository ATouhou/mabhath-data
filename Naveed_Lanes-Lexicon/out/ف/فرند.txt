فرندفِرِنْدٌ A sort of cloth, or garment, (Lth, T, O,
K,) well known: (K:) an adventitious word,
(Lth, T, O,) [from the Pers. پَرَنْد,] arabicized.
(O, K.) ―
-b2-  And [hence, app.,] فِرِنْدُ السَّيْفِ, (T,
S, M, O, K,) and ↓ إِفْرِنْدُهُ, (S, O, K,) The diversified wavy marks, streaks, grain, or water, of
the sword; syn. وَشْيُهُ, (T, S, M, O, K,) and
جَوْهَرُهُ, (T, O, K,) and رُبَدُهُ [q. v.], (S, O,) and
مَاؤُهُ الَّذِى يَجْرِى فِيهِ, (T, O, *) and طَرَائِقُهُ, (T, O,)
i. e. سَفَاسِقُهُ. (T.) ―
-b3-  And الفِرِنْدُ signifies The
sword itself. (M, L, K.) ―
-b4-  And The حَوْجَم;
(K, TA;) i. e. (TA) the red rose. (M, TA.) ―
-b5-
And The grains of the pomegranate. (AA,
O, K.)
