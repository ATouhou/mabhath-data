كدش1
كَدَشَ لِعِيَالِهِ, aor.
كَدِشَ, (S, K,) inf. n. كَدْشٌ,
(TK,) He sought, sought after, or sought to gain,
sustenance; or he gained, acquired, or earned;
syn. كَدَحَ, (S, K,) and كَسَبَ; (K;) and collected; and exercised art, cunning, or skill;
(TA;) for his family, or household. (S, K.)
―
-b2-  You say also, كَدَشْتُ مِنْ فُلَانٍ شَيْئًا, ('Okbeh
Es-Sulamee, TA,) or عَطَآءً, (S, K,) I obtained
from such a one ('Okbeh, S, K) a thing,
('Okbeh,) or a gift; (S, K;) as also ↓ أَكْدَشْتُ,
(K, and so in a copy of the S,) or ↓ إِكْتَدَشْتُ.
('Okbeh, as related by Aboo-Turáb; and so in
two copies of the S.) And مَا كَدَشَ مِنْهُ شَيْئًا
He did not obtain, and did not take, of him
anything. (TA.)
