تَكَأَّدَنِى and ↓ تَكَآءَدَنِى It (a thing, or an

troubled, fatigued, or wearied, me. (S, L, K.)

-b2-  تكأّد الشَّىْءَ He took, or imposed, upon himself,
or undertook, the thing, in spite of difficulty,
trouble or inconvenience; he constrained himself
to do the thing, notwithstanding it was difficult,
troublesome, or inconvenient, to him. (L, K.)
You say تَكَأَّدْتُ الذَّهَابَ إِلَى فُلَانٍ, meaning, I
[constrained myself to go, and] went to such a
one, in spite of difficulty, trouble, or inconvenience.
(AZ, L.) ―
-b3-  He endured the thing; struggled
with, or against, it; contended with its difficulty,
or severity; underwent difficulties, troubles, or
inconveniences, in doing it; endured, or bore, its
heat and severity; syn. كَابَدَهُ, and صَلِىَ بِهِ.
(IAar, L, K.)

-b2-  Grief;

-b3-  Injustice. (K.) ―
-b4-  A dark night. (IAar, L, K.)

-b5-  See also كَؤُودٌ.
عَقَبَةٌ كَؤُودٌ, (S, L, K,) and ↓ كَأْدَآءُ, (L, K,)
A mountain-road difficult of ascent. (S, L, K. *)
Such a road is also termed [simply] كَأْدَآءُ, and
صُعَدَآءُ: [in the K it is said, that كُؤَدَآءُ is syn.
with صُعَدَآءُ:] and كَؤُودٌ [in like manner, as a
subst.,] signifies. a difficult place of ascent: like
صَعُودٌ. (L, TA.)
