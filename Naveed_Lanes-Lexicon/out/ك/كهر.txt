كهر1
كَهَرَهُ, aor.
كَهَرَ, inf. n. كَهْرٌ, He chid him with
rough speech, (S, Mgh, K,) to show him contempt. (TA.) ―
-b2-  He reviled him. (Az, TA.) ―
-b3-
He encountered him with a frowning face, (K,)
to show him contempt: (TA:) or he frowned at
him. (TA.) ―
-b4-  He oppressed him; i. q. قَهَرَهُ.
(S, K.) So in the Kur, [xciii. 9,] فَأَمَّا ا@لْيَتِيمَ فَلَا
تكْهَرْ [Therefore, as to the orphan, thou shalt not
oppress him]; accord. to the reading of Ibn-Mes'ood. (Ks, S.) Yaakoob says, that the ك in
كَهَرَهُ is a substitute for the ق in قَهَرَهُ. (TA.)
