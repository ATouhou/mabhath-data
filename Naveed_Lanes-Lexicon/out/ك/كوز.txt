كُوزٌ A kind of vessel, (TA,) well-known, (A,
K,) [namely, a mug, or drinking-cup,] with a
handle: (IAar, TA:) AHn says, that it is a
Persian word; but ISd denies this, and asserts
it to be genuine Arabic: it is said to be from
كَازَ “ he collected: ” (TA:) pl. [of pauc.] أَكْوَازٌ,
and [of mult.] كِيزَانٌ and كِوَزَةٌ. (S, K.)
كَوَّازَةٌ [app. A stand, or a shelf, upon which
mugs (كِيزَان) are placed: see بَرَّادَةٌ]. (Lth, T,
art. برد.)
رَجُلٌ مُكَوَّزُ الرَّأْسِ A man having a long head.
(A, K.)
