كشثكَشُوثٌ (S, K) and كُشُوثٌ and كَشُوثَى and
كَشُوثَاءُ (of the fem. gender, Ibn-Buzruj, in TA,
voce هِنْدَبٌ) and أُكْشُوثٌ but this last is a bad
word, (K,) [as also شَكُوثَى and شَكُوثَاءُ,] [A
species of cuscuta, or dodder;] a certain plant
that clings to the branches of trees, having no
root in the earth. (S, K.) [See also الشَّجَرَةُ
الخَبِيثَةُ, in art. خبث. And see الفَقْدُ and سَكَرٌ.]
―
-b2-  A poet says,
هُوَ الكَشُوثُ فَلَا أَصْلٌ وَلَا وَرَقٌ
وَلَا نَسِيمٌ وَلَا ظِلٌّ وَلَا ثَمَرُ
[He is the Kashooth: therefore (he has) no root
nor leaves nor fragrance, nor shade nor fruit].
(S.)
