صعترصَعْتَرٌ I. q. سَعْتَرٌ [q. v.]: (K, and S and Msb in
art. سعتر:) it grows in the country of the Arabs,
and is of two kinds, سُهْلِىٌّ [i. e. of the plain] and
جَبَلِىٌّ [i. e. of the mountain]: (AHn, TA:) when
strewn in a place, it drives away venomous or
noxious reptiles and the like, (K,) such as serpents
and scorpions. (TA.)
