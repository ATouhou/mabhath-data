حتد1
حَتَدَ بِمَكَانٍ, aor.
حَتِدَ, (S, L, K,) inf. n. حَتْدٌ,
(L,) He remained, stayed, abode, or dwelt, in
a place, (S, L, K,) and became fixed, or settled.
(S, L.)
-A2-  حَتِدَ, aor.
حَتَدَ, (L, K,) inf. n. حَتَدٌ, (L,)
It (anything, L, K) was pure, (L,) or pure in
origin. (K.)
2
حتّدهُ, inf. n. تَحْتِيدٌ, He chose it for its
purity and excellence. (K.)
حَتِدٌ Anything (L, K) pure, (L,) or pure in
origin. (K.)
مَحِْتدٌ Origin; syn. أَصْلٌ; (IAar, S, L, K;) app.
in respect of race, or lineage, only, as several of
the lexicologists have expressly asserted; (MF;)
as also مَحْفِدٌ (S, L) and مَحْقِدٌ and مَحْكِدٌ:
(IAar, L:) pl. مَحَاتِدُ. (A.) You say, فُلَانٌ مِنْ
مَحْتِدِ صِدْقٍ, (S,) or فِى مَحْتِدِ صِدْقٍ, (A,) [Such
a one is of a good, or an excellent, origin.] And
هُوَ كَرِيمُ المَحْتِدِ [He is generous in respect of
origin]. (A, L.) ―
-b2-  Also Nature; natural, or
native, disposition, temper, or the like. (L, K.)
You say, of a man who has done an act of kindness and reverted from it, رَجَعَ إِلَى مَحْتِدِهِ He
returned to his natural disposition. (L.)
