حَوْشَبٌ The fetlock-joint (مَوْصِلُ الوَظِيفِ) in
the pastern (رُسْغ) of a beast: (S, K:) or, (K,)
as also ↓ حشيب and ↓ حشيبى, (so in the TA,) a
bone in the inside of the hoof, between the tendons
(عَصَب) and the وَظِيف [or shank; app. the lower
pastern-bone]: (K:) or the contents (حَشْو) of
the hoof: (AA, TA:) or a small bone, like a
سُلَامَى [or finger-bone, a description aptly applying to either of the pastern-bones, the upper of
which seems to be here meant], at the extremity
of the وَظِيف, between the head thereof and the
place where the hoof is set on, (As, S, K,) entering
into the جُبَّة: (As, S: [see this last word (جبّة),
to which various significations are assigned; here
said in the TA to be that which contains the
حوشب and دَخِيس (both of which words seem to
be syn.), between, or amid, the flesh and the
tendons:]) or the bone of the رُسْغ [or pastern]:
(T, K:) or a name applied to each of the two
bones of the pastern (رسغ) of a horse. (TA.)
