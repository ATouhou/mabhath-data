بنفسجبَنَفْسَجٌ, of the measure قَعَلَّلٌ, like سَفَرْجَلٌ,
(Msb,) [an arabicized word, from the Persian
بَنَفْشَ; The violet; viola odorata of Linn: and
accord. to Forskål (Flora AEgypt. Arab. p. ciii.)
applied in El-Yemen to the “ iris: ” and (p. cxx.)
“ tagetes dubia? ”] what is thus called is well
known: the smelling it in its fresh state is beneficial to those who are heated by wrath (المَحْرُورِين),
and the continual smelling of it induces good sleep:
the conserve made of it is beneficial for the pleurisy
(ذَاتُ الجَنْبِ), and for inflammation of the lungs
(ذَاتُ الرِّئَةِ), and for cough, and for headache.
(K.)
