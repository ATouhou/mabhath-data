بربخبَرْبَخٌ The passage, or conduit, of water, called
إِرْدَبَّة and بَالُوعَة [q. v.], made of baked clay: (K:)
or بَرَابِخُ [the pl.] signifies the baked-clay conduits
of privies, which convey [the water &c.] from the
house-top to the ground. (S, but omitted in some
copies.) ―
-b2-  برْبَخُ البَوْلِ The canal of the urine
[from the kidney to the bladder; i. e. the ureter]:
(L, KL, TA:) of the dial. of Egypt. (TA.)
