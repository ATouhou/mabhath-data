the dial. of El-Hijáz called طِبِّيخٌ, (Msb,) A
certain well-known fruit; (Msb;) [the melon,

particularly, the water-melon; cucurbita citrullus:
or a plant] of the kind called يَقْطِين, that does
not grow tall, but extends itself upon the surface
of the ground: (K, TA:) and also the خِرْبِز [or
خَرْبُز, a Persian word, and applied to the water-melon, by the Turks termed by this name, and

found by me in my MS. copy of the K, nor in

خَرْبُز: (Mgh:) [the term بطّيخ is applied to
many varieties of the water-melon, distinguished
