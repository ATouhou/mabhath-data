بَطٌّ A kind of water-fowl; (S, O, Msb;) [the
duck, or ducks; and the goose, or geese; but
generally the former of these birds; agreeably
with a statement in the Jm, that بَطٌّ is applied by
the Arabs to the small, and إِوَزٌّ to the large;]
i. q. إِوَزٌّ, (K, TA,) both the small thereof and the
large: (TA:) a Persian word (عَجَمِىٌّ), arabicized;
[originally بَتْ, or بَطْ, or بَطّ;] or, accord. to
IJ, an imitation of its cries: n. un. بَطَّةٌ, (S, Msb,
K,) which is applied to the male and to the female,
(S, Msb,) like حَمَامَةٌ and دَجَاجَةٌ: (S:) pl. بِطَاطٌ.
(TA.)
بَطَّةٌ n. un. of بَطٌّ, q. v. ―
