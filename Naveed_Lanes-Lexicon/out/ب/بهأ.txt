بهأ1
بَهَأ بِهِ, and بَهِئَ, (AZ, S, Mgh, K,) [aor.
بَهَاَ,]
and بَهُؤَ, (K,) [aor.
بَهُاَ] inf. n. بَهْءٌ and بُهُوْءٌ (AZ,
S, K) and بَهَآءٌ; (K;) and به ↓ ابتهأ; (Aboo-Sa'eed, TA;) He was, or became, sociable, friendly, or familiar, with him, or it; (AZ, S, Mgh, K;)
namely, a man, (AZ, S,) or a thing; (Mgh;) and
loved, or liked, his, or its, nearness: (Aboo-Sa'eed,
TA:) and he became familiar with it so as to
have little, or no, reverence for it, or awe of it.
(Mgh, TA.) بَهَوْا بِهِ occurs in a trad., as they
relate it, for بَهَؤُوا به: (A 'Obeyd, TA:) and
↓ يَبْتَهِى, in a verse of El-Aashà, for يَبْتَهِئُ. (As,
O, TS, L.) ―
-b2-  مَا بَهَأْتُ لَهُ I did not understand
it; or I did not know it; (ISk, S, K;) as also
مَا بَأَهْتُ لَهُ. (ISk, S.)
