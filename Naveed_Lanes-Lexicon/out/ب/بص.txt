R.Q.1
بَصْبَصَ, (S, K,) or بَصْبَصَ بِذَنَبِهِ, (M,)
inf. n. بَصْبَصَةٌ, (TA,) He (a dog, S, M, K, and a
beast of prey, and a gazelle, and a camel when
urged on by the driver's singing, TA) wagged, or
moved about, his tail; (S, K;) which a dog does
by reason of cupidity, or fear; (TA;) as also
↓ تَبَصْبَصَ: (S:) or he (a dog) struck with his
tail. (ISd.) The inf. n. بَصْبَصَةٌ has a pl., namely,
بَصَابِصُ; as in the following ex.:
حَتَّى إِذَا أَبْصَرْنَهُ وَ عَلِمْنَهُ
حَيَّيْنَهُ بِبَصَابِصِ الأَذْنَابِ
[Until, when they see him and know him, they
greet him with waggings of the tails]. (TA.) It
is said in a prov., respecting the flight and submissiveness of the coward,
بَصْبَصْنَ إِذْ حُدِيْنَ بِالأَذْنَابِ
[They wagged the tails when they were urged on
by the driver's singing]. (As.)
-A2-  بَصْبَصَتِ الإِبِلُ
قَرَبَهَا The camels performed quickly their nightjourney to water. (K.) [See بَصْبَاصٌ.]
