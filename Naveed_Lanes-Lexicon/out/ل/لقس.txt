لقس1
لَقِسَتْ نَفْسُهُ, (S, A, K,) aor.
لَقَسَ, (S, K,) inf. n.
لَقَسٌ, (S, TA,) His soul [or stomach] heaved; or
became agitated by a tendency to vomit; or
became heavy; syn. غَثَتْ, [q. v.,] (S, A, K,)
and خَبُثَتْ; (S, K;) مِنَ الشَّىْءِ in consequence of
the thing. (S, K.) Mohammad desired his followers to use this expression instead of خَبُثَتْ
نَفْسُهُ, which he disliked. (K, TA.) ―
-b2-  With
إِلى الشَّىْءِ following it, His soul strove with him
to incline him to the thing, (K,) and became
greedy for it. (TA.) [But Az seems to disapprove of this explanation.]
لَقِسَةٌ, as an epithet applied to نَفْسٌ, is the
part. n. of لَقِسَتْ in the [first and] second of the
senses explained above. (TA.)
