Msb, in the state of إِحْرَام, S, L,) put upon his

or lousy, (A 'Obeyd, L,) during the state of احرام.
(S, L.) The Arabs in the time of paganism used to
do thus when they did not desire to shave their
heads during the pilgrimage. Some say, that it
signifies He shaved the whole of his hair. (L.)

لَبَدٌ Wool. (S, K.) Hence the saying مَا لَهُ
سَبَدٌ وَلَا لَبَدٌ He has neither hair nor wool: (S:)
or, neither what has hair nor what has wool: or,

لبد [app. لَبِدٌ] Compact, or cohering, ground,
upon which one may walk, or journey, quickly.
(L.)
لَبِدٌ (S, K) and ↓ لُبَدٌ, (S, A, L, K,) the former
of which is preferable, accord. to A'Obeyd, (S,)
(tropical:)  One who does not travel, (S, L,) nor quit his
abode, (S, * L, K,) or place, (A,) nor seek sustenance.
(L, K.) Hence, (A,) the last of Lukmán's [seven]
vultures [with whose life his own was to terminate]
was called ↓ لُبَدٌ, (S, A, L, K,) because he thought
that is would not go away nor die. (L.) Thus
applied, it is perfectly decl., because it is a word
not made to deviate from its original form.
(S, L.) ―
