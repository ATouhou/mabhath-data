لسب1
لَسَبَهُ, aor.
لَسِبَ, (S, K,) and
لَسَبَ, (K,) inf. n.
لَسْبٌ, (S,) It (a scorpion, S, or a serpent or
other thing, K, as a scorpion, and wasp, but
generally said of a scorpion, TA,) stung him, or
bit him. (S, K.) ―
-b2-  لَسَبَهُ أَسْوَاطًا He flogged him
with whips. (S.) لَسَبَهُ بِالسَّوْطِ He flogged him
with the whip. (K.) ―
-b3-  لَسِبَ, aor.
لَسَبَ, (S, K,)
inf. n. لَسْبٌ, (S,) He licked honey, (S, K,) or
the like, (K,) as clarified butter. (TA.)
-A2-
لَسِبَ بِهِ, aor.
لَسَبَ, It adhered, clave, or stuck, to it,
or in it: (S, K:) like لَصِبَ. (S.)
لَسْبَةٌ, like لَعْقَةٌ, A single lick of honey or the
like. (TA.)
مَا تَرَكَ لَسُوبًا, and ↓ لَسُّوبًا, He left not anything:
[lit., what might be licked off]. (K.) [See also
كَسُوبٌ.]
لَسُّوبًا: see لَسُوبًا.
