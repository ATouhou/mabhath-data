دعمصQ. 1
دَعْمَصَ It (water) abounded with دَعَامِيص
[pl. of دُعْمُوصٌ]. (K.)
دُعْمُوصٌ A certain animalcule (دُوَيْبَّةٌ), (S, K,)
that dives in water: (S:) or a certain black
animalcule that swims upon water: (Mgh:) or a
certain worm (دُودَةٌ), that is in pools left by
torrents when their water sinks into the earth:
(IDrd, K:) or a certain worm (دودة) having
two heads, seen in water when it becomes little in
quantity: (IB:) pl. دَعَامِيصُ and دَعَامِصُ. (S.)
―
-b2-  [Hence, app.,] (assumed tropical:)  One who enters much into
affairs; who is a frequent visitor of kings. (K.)
And hence, الأَطْفَالُ دَعَامِيصُ الجَنَّةِ, meaning
(assumed tropical:)  [Infants will be] roamers in Paradise: they
will not be debarred from any dwelling: (K:)
a trad.: but the words occurring in a trad. of
Aboo-Hureyreh are صِغَارُكُمْ دَعَامِيصُ الجَنَّةِ
[Your little children &c.]. (TA.) ―
-b3-  Also The
embryo in the belly of a mare until the fortieth
day: then its make becomes apparent, and it is
called دُودَةٌ, until three months old: when it is
called سَلِيلٌ. (Kr, TA.)
دُعَيْمِيصٌ [dim. of دُعْمُوصٌ]. ―
-b2-  One says, هُوَ
دُعَيْمِيصُ هٰذَا الأَمْرِ, meaning He is acquainted
with, or knowing in, this affair. (S, K.) دُعَيْمِيصُ
الرَّمْلِ was the name of a certain cunning, or knowing, or skilful, man; and hence the saying above
mentioned: (S:) he was a black slave, very cunning or knowing or skilful, and an expert guide of
the way. (K.) And [hence] one says, أَهْدَى مِنْ
دُعَيْمِيصُ الرَّمْلِ [More expert in showing the way
than Do'eymees-er-Raml]. (O, TA.)
