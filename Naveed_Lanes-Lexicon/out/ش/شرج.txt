شَرْجٌ A place in which water flows from a
[stony tract such as is termed] حَرَّة to a soft, or
plain, tract; (S, K;) as also ↓ شَرْجَةٌ: (TA:) or
the latter signifies [simply] a place in which water
flows; and some elide the ة, saying شَرْجٌ: (Msb:)
pl. شِرَاجٌ (S, Mgh, Msb, K, expl. in the Mgh

(S, K.)
-A2-  Also A party, or distinct body or
class [of men]. (S, K.) One says, أَصْبَحُوا فِى
هٰذَا الأَمْرِ شَرْجَيْنِ i. e. [They became, in this
affair,] two parties. (S.) And it is said in a
trad., أَصْبَحَ النَّاسُ شَرْجَيْنِ فِى السَّفَرِ [The people,

meaning, half of them fasting, and half of them
breaking the fast. (TA.) ―
-b2-  And The like of

this. (S.) ―
