زُقٌّ one of the names of Wine: (Moheet, K: *)
pl., as in the Moheet, زِقَقَةٌ; but accord. to the K,
زَقَقَةٌ. (TA.)
زِقٌّ [A skin for holding wine &c.:] any receptacle, consisting of a skin, that is used for
wine and the like: or, as some say, not thus called
unless it be stripped off from the part next the
animal's neck: or, accord. to AHn, one in which
wine is conveyed: (M:) or a skin for water or
milk; syn. سِقَآءٌ: (S, K:) or a skin of which the
hair is clipped, not plucked out, (Lth, K,) for
wine and the like, (Lth,) or for wine &c.: (K:)
or a receptacle, (ظَرْفٌ, Msb, and Har p. 335,)
of skin, in which are put clarified butter and
vinegar and wine: (Har ibid.:) or, as some say,
a ظَرْف smeared with زِفْت: (Msb:) AHát says
that it is such as is smeared with زِفْت or with
قِير: (TA:) pl. (of pauc., S) أَزْقَاقٌ (S, M, Msb, K)
and أَزُقٌّ, mentioned by El-Hejeree, (M,) and (of
mult., S) زِقَاقٌ (S, M, K) and زُقَّانٌ [in the CK
erroneously written رُقّاقٌ]. (S, M, Msb, K, TA.)
زُقَّةٌ A certain small bird; (K;) a certain
aquatic bird, that remains still until it is almost
seized, and then dives, and comes forth far off:
pl. زُقَقٌ. (M.)
زَقَاقٌ: see زَقَّاقٌ.
زُقَاقٌ A سِكَّة [meaning street]: (S, K:) or

Mgh, Msb,) whether a thoroughfare or not:
(Mgh, Msb:) masc. (S, Msb) and fem.: (S,
Msb, K:) Akh says that the people of El-Hijáz
make الطَّرِيقُ and الصِّرَاطُ (S, Msb) and السَّبِيلُ (S)

is the market of El-Basrah, (S,) fem.; and Temeem make them masc., (S, Msb,) i. e. all of
these: (S:) pl. [of pauc., but also used as a pl. of
mult.,] أَزِقَّةٌ (S, M, Mgh, Msb, K) and [of mult.]
زُقَّانٌ. (Sb, S, M, K.) مَنْ هَدَىزُقَاقًا, occurring

and the blind to his way. (TA.) ―
