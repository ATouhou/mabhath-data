زبعالزَّوْبَعَةٌ The name of a certain devil; (Lth, K;)
to which some add, insolent and audacious in
pride and rebellion: (TA:) or a certain chief of
the jinn, or genii: (S, K:) said to be one of those,
nine or seven in number, spoken of in the Kur-án
[xlvi. 28], as listening to the Kur-án. (TA.) ―
-b2-
And hence, زَوْبَعَةٌ, (S, K,) and (K,) or as some
say, (S, TA,) أُمُّ زَوْبَعَةٍ, (S, and so in some copies
of the K,) or أُمُّ زَوْبَعَةَ, (as in other copies of the
K,) and, (K,) as the children of the Arabs of the
desert call it, (Lth,) أَبُو زَوْبَعَةٍ, or أَبُو زَوْبَعَةَ,
(accord. to different copies of the K,) i. q. إِعْصَارٌ;
(Lth, S, K;) i. e. A whirlwind of dust [or sand]
rising into the sky; (TA;) a wind that raises the
dust [or sand] and rises towards the sky as though
it were a pillar: (S:) [I have measured several
of these whirling pillars of dust or sand, with a
sextant, in circumstances peculiarly favourable to
accuracy, in Upper Egypt, and found them from
five hundred to seven hundred and fifty feet in
height:] it is said [in the present day] that in the
زوبعة is a devil, insolent and audacious in pride
and rebellion. (K.) ―
-b3-  زَوَابِعُ [is the pl., and also]
signifies Calamities, or misfortunes. (TA.)
