زحلقQ. 1
زَحْلَقَةٌ [inf. n. of زَحْلَقَ] The rolling [a
thing] down; syn. دَحْرَجَةٌ. (S, K.) You say,
زَحْلَقَ الحَجَرَ, i. e. دَحْرَجَهُ [He rolled down the
stone]. (TK.)
Q. 2
تَزَحْلَقَ He, or it, rolled down; syn.
تَدَحْرَجَ: (S, K:) or he slid, or slipped, upon his
posteriors. (TA.)
زِحْلِقٌ A violent wind. (Ibn-'Abbád, K.)
زِحْلِيقٌ: see the next paragraph.
زُحْلُوقَةٌ i. q. زُحْلُوفَةٌ; (S, * K;) meaning [A
sloping slide, or rolling-place; or] the traces of
the sliding of children from above to below:
(TA:) of the dial. of Temeem: pl. زَحَالِيقُ: (S:)
which likewise signifies slippery places; as also
↓ زِحْلِيقٌ. (TA.) ―
-b2-  And (tropical:)  A grave: (K, TA:)
because one slips into it. (TA.) ―
-b3-  And i. q.
أُرْجُوحَةٌ; i. e. [A seesaw;] a piece of wood [or a
plank] which children lay upon an elevated place,
and a number sit upon one end of it, and a number upon the other [end]; and when one of the
