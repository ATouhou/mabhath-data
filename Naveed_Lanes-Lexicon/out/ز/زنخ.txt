زَنِخَ, aor.
زَنَخَ (S, K,) inf. n. زَنَخٌ, (S,) said of
oil, (S K,) and clarified butter, (JK, L,) and
food, (L,) It was, or became, altered [for the
worse] (S, K, TA) in odour; (TA;) [stinking,
rancid,] bad, or corrupt; like سَنِخَ. (JK.) ―
-b2-
And, said of a lamb, or kid, He raised his head
in sucking, by reason of choking, or of dryness
of the fauces. (K.)
زَنِخٌ, applied to oil, (S, K,) and clarified butter,
and food, (L,) Altered [for the worse] (S, K, TA)
in odour; (TA;) [stinking, rancid, bad, or corrupt; like سَنِخٌ: see 1.] ―
-b2-  إِبِلٌ زَنِخَةٌ Camels
