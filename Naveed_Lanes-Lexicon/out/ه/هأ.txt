هأR. Q. 1
هَأْهَأَ بِالإِبِلِ, (El-Umawee, S, K,) inf. n.
هِيهَآءٌ and هَأْهَآءٌ, (K,) the latter extr., (TA,) [see
ظَأْظَأَ,] He called the camels to food, or provender,
by the cry هِئْ هِئْ: (S, K:) or he chid them,
(زَجَرَهَا), by the cry هَأْهَأْ. (K.) [See also arts.
جأ and جيأ.] ―
-b2-  هَأْهَأَ, inf. n. هَأْهَآءٌ, He chid a dog.
(TA.) ―
-b3-  He called a dog. (TA.) ―
-b4-  هَأْهَأَ, inf.
n. هَأْهَآءٌ, He laughed loud and long: [a word imitative of the sound]. (K, TA.)
هِىْءٌ, subst. from هَأْهَأَ, [A call to camels to food,
or provender]. (S, K.) هِىْءٌ and جِىْءٌ are said
to be thus written by Az's own hand, with kesr:
and are thus written in the Jáme' [of Kz]. (L.)
[See arts. جأ and جيأ.]
هَأْهَأٌ and هَأْهَآءٌ One who laughs loud and long.
(K, TA.) ―
-b2-  جَارِيَةٌ هَأْهَأَةٌ A damsel who laughs
loud and long. (Lh, TA.)
