هدج1
هَدَجَ, aor.
هَدِجَ, inf. n. هَدَجَانٌ (S, K) and
هُدَاجٌ (K) and هَدْجٌ, (TA,) (tropical:)  He walked gently,
in a weak manner: (TA:) or he walked in the
manner of an old man; (S, K;) and the like:
(TA:) or he (an old man) walked with short
steps: or walked hastily, without desiring to do so:
(TA:) or he walked with unintermitting steps:
(As:) or he walked in a shaking manner, by
reason of old age. (IAar.) ―
-b2-  هَدَجَ, (S,) aor.
هَدِجَ,
inf. n. هَدَجَانٌ; and ↓ استهدج; (TA;) He (an
ostrich) walked, or went, (or ran, TA,) in a
tremulous manner. (S, TA.) ―
-b3-  هَدَجَتِ القِدْرُ
(tropical:)  The cooking-pot boiled vehemently. (TA.) ―
-b4-
هَدَجَتْ, (S,) inf. n. هَدَجٌ, (L,) She (a camel)
yearned towards her young one; حَنَّتْ عَلَى وَلَدِهَا;
(S, L;) as also ↓ تهدّجت; (L;) [and, app.,
uttered the cry produced by yearning towards her
young one: see below:] or the latter signifies she
was affectionate to her young one. (S, K.) ―
-b5-
