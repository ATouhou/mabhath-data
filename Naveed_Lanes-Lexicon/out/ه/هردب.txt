هردبQ. 1
هَرْدَبَ, inf. n. هَرْدَبَةٌ, (and هردب, TA, [a
strange form: perhaps a mistake for هَرْدَابٌ or
هِرْدَابٌ:]) He ran heavily. (IKtt &c., and K.)
هِرْدَبَّةٌ (and هِرْدَبٌّ, TA) An old woman. (S,
K.) ―
-b2-  Also, (as some say, TA,) Having a
swollen belly, and cowardly: (S, K:) or cowardly,
bulky, of little sense, swollen in the belly, and having
no heart. (TA.) ―
-b3-  Accord. to Az, in the T, A
large, long-bodied man is called هِرْدَبَّةٌ. (TA.)
