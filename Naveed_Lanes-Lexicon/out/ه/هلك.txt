هلك1
هَلَكَ
, inf. n. هَلَاكٌ &c., (S, K, &c.,) He, or
it, perished, came to nought, came to an end,
passed away, was not, was no more, or became
non-existent or annihilated: (KL, PS in explanation of هَلاَكٌ, &c.:) or fell: or became in a
bad, or corrupt, state; became corrupted, vitiated,
marred, or spoiled: or went away, no one knew
whither: (Mgh in explanation of هَلاَكٌ:) he died.
(K.) ―
-b2-  هَلَكَتْ أَرْضُهُ
His land had its herbage
dried up by drought: see جَرِبَ.
