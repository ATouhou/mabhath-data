fluency; syn. سَرَدَ (S, L, K) and تَابَعَ. (L.) ―

هَتٌّ signifies a woman's spinning thread continuously. (K.)

هَتْهَتَ He urged a camel (زَجَرَهُ) on
the occasion of drinking, by the cry هَتْ هَتْ.
(AHeyth, K.) See هَتْ, below. ―
-b2-  هَتْهَتَ, inf. n.
هَتْهَتَةٌ; as also تَهْتَهَ; He twisted, or distorted, his
tongue in speaking. (Az.) ―
-b3-  Also هتهت; (and
↓ هَتَّ, TA, [aor.
هَتِ3َ?];) He was quick, or rapid,
in his speech. (K.) ―
-b4-  See هَتَّ.
هَتْ, (TA,) or هَتْ هَتْ, (K,) A cry by which
a camel is urged (يُزْجَرُ) on the occasion of drinking. (K.) ―
-b2-  إِذَا وَقَّفْتَ البَعِيرَ عَلَى الرَّدْهَةِ فَلَا
تَقُلْ لَهُ هَتْ; or, as some say, فَلَا تُهَتْهِتْ بِهِ;
[When thou hast made the camel to stand over
the hollow in the rock in which the rain-water
has collected, say not to him هَتْ]. A proverb;
meaning, accord. to AHeyth, when thou hast
shewn a man his right course of conduct, do not
urge him. (TA.) ―
-b3-  هَتُّ قَوَائِمِ البَعِيرِ The sound
of the falling of the camel's feet [upon the
ground]. (L.)
-A2-  تَرَكَهُمْ هَتًّا بَتًّا [He left

pieces. (L.)
