اَزِمَ, inf. n. أَزْمٌ and أُزُومٌ, He bit with
the whole mouth, vehemently: (K:) or with the
canine teeth: or you say, أَزَمَهُ, and أَزَمَ عَلَيْهِ,
meaning he bit it, and then repeated [the action]
upon it, not letting it go: or he seized upon it
with his mouth: (TA:) or أَزَمَهُ signifies [simply]
he bit it: (S:) and أَزَمَ عَلَيْهِ, aor.
اَزِمَ, inf. n. أَزْمٌ;
and أَزِمَ, aor.
اَزَمَ, inf. n. أَزَمٌ; the same; or he
seized, or took hold, upon it with his teeth: (Msb:)
and أَزَمْتُ يَدَ الرَّجُلِ I bit the arm, or hand, of the
man most vehemently. (TA.) أَزَمَ بِهَا occurs in a
trad. as meaning He bit it, (referring to a ring of
a coat of mail,) and held it between two of his
central teeth. (AO.) And in another trad., أَزَمَ
فِي يَدِهِ, meaning He bit his arm, or hand. (TA.)
And you say, أَزَمَ الفَرَسُ عَلَى فَأْسِ اللِّجَامِ The
horse seized [with his teeth, or champed,] upon the
فأس [q. v.] of the bit. (K.) And أَزْمٌ signifies
also The cutting with the canine tooth, and with
a knife, (K,) and with other things. (TA.) ―

(TA:) or, in this instance, (TA,) الأَزْمُ signifies
