مَأْبُورٌ A dog that has had a needle given him,
to eat, in bread: (S:) and, with ة, applied to a
sheep or goat (شاة) that has eaten a needle in its
fodder, and in whose inside it has stuck fast; in
consequence of which the animal eats nothing, or,
if it eat, the eating does it no good. (TA.) It is
said in a trad., المُؤْمِنُ كَالْكَلْبِ المَأْبُورِ The believer
is like the dog that has had a needle given to him,
to eat, in bread. (S.) [Accord. to Ibr D, the
meaning is, that he is generous and incautious, so
that he is easily deceived.]
