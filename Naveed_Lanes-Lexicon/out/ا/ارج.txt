ارج1
أَرِجَ, aor.
اَرَجَ, inf. n. أَرَجٌ (S, A, Msb, K) and
أَرِيجٌ (S, A, K) and أَرِيجَةٌ, (K, [in which it is only
mentioned as syn. with the first and second of
these ns., so that it may be a simple subst.,]) It
(perfume) diffused, or exhaled, its odour; (S, A;)
as also ↓ تآرّج: (A:) it had a hot, or strong,
odour; syn. تَوَهَّجَ رِيحُهُ. (S, A, K.) ―
-b2-  It (a
place) was, or became, strongly fragrant. (Msb.)
-A2-  أَرَجَ: see 2, in three places.

-b2-
