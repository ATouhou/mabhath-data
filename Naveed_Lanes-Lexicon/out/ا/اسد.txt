أَسِدَ, (S, M, A, K,) aor.
اَسَدَ, (K,) inf. n. أَسَدٌ,
(TA,) (tropical:)  He (a man, M) was, or became, like a
lion, (S, M, A, K,) in his boldness, (A,) and his
other dispositions; (S, A, TA;) as also ↓ استأسد;
(M, A, K;) [and ↓ تأسّد; (see أَسِدٌ;)] عَلَيْهِ towards him, or against him. (A.) You say
أَسَدٌ بَيِّنُ الأَسَدِ [A lion bearing evidence of being
like a lion in boldness]: an extr. phrase, like
حِقَّةٌ بَيِنَّةٌ الحِّقَةِ; (TA;) which is [said to be] the
only other instance of the kind. (TA in art. حق.)
[Hence the saying,] إِذَا دَخَلَ فَهِدَ وَ إِذَا خَرَجَ أَسِدَ
(tropical:)  [When he comes in, he is like a lynx; and when
he goes out, he is like a lion: see فَهِدَ]. (S, from
a trad.) You say also, أَسِدَ عَلَيْهِ meaning (assumed tropical:)  He
became emboldened against him; (TA;) as also
↓ استأسد. (S, Msb, K.) And (assumed tropical:)  He was, or became, angry with him: (M, L, K: *) or (so
accord. to the M and L, but in the K, “and,”)
behaved in a light and hasty manner, or foolishly,
or ignorantly, towards him. (M, L, K.*) ―
-b2-
أَسِدَ, (S, K,) aor. as above, (K,) and so the inf. n.,
(TA,) also signifies (assumed tropical:)  He (a man, S) became
stupified (S, K) by fear (S) at seeing a lion. (S,
K.) Thus it has two contr. meanings. (K.)
