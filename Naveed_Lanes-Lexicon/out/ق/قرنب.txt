قرنبقَرْنَبٌ The jerboa; or a rat, or mouse; syn.
قَأْرَةٌ: or the young one generated between it and a
jerboa: (K:) and فرنب [i. e. فِرْنِبٌ, with kesr,]
is a dial. var. thereof. (So in the TA. [But I
incline to think that قَرْنَبٌ is a mistranscription
for فِرْنِبٌ.])
قُرْنُبٌ The flank: (IAar, O, K:) or a flabby
flank. (TA.)
قَرَنْبَى An insect resembling the [beetle called]
خُنْفَسَآء, or somewhat larger than the latter, with
long hind-legs: (As, T, TA:) or an insect with long
hind-legs, resembling the خنفسآء, (S, O, Meyd,)
but somewhat larger, (S, O,) having a speckled
back. (Meyd.) It is said in prov., القَرَنْبَى فِى
عَيْنِ أُمِّهَا حَسَنَةٌ [The karembà in the eye of its
mother is beautiful]. (S, O, Meyd. [Mentioned
in the S and O, in art. قرب.]) [See اِبْنُ الفَاسِيَآءِ,
in art. فسو.]
