قلقسقُلْقَاسٌ [The colocasia; or arum. colocasia of
Linnæus: or its root:] the root of a certain
plant, which is eaten cooked, (AHn, K,) and used
medicinally: (AHn:) the decoction thereof increases the venereal faculty, and fattens; but the
taking it constantly engenders black bile. (AHn,
K.) [See De Sacy's “ Relation de l'Egypte par
Abd-allatif,” pp. 94 — 98.]
