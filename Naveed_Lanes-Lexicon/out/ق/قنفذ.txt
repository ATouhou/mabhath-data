قنفذمَا أَبْيَضُ شَطْرًا أَسْوَدُ ظَهْرًا يَمْشِى قِمْطَرًا وَيَبُولُ
قَطْرًا
What is white in one half, black in the
back, that walks contractedly, and makes water
in drops? An enigma: to which the answer is, The
قُنْفُذ [q. v.]. (L.) ―
-b2-  قُنْفُذُ لَيْلٍ
One who sleeps not
[during the night]; because the قنفذ sleeps not:
(L:) and a calumniator; a slanderer; (L, K;)
as also أَنْقَدُ لَيْلٍ. (L.) ―
-b3-  Also قُنْفُذٌ and قُنْفَذٌ
The rat, or mouse; syn. فَارٌ: (K:) fem. with ة.
(TA.) ―
-b4-  قُنْفُذٌ (assumed tropical:)  The part behind the ear of a
camel, whence the sweat exudes. (S, L, K.) ―
-b5-
And, with ة, (assumed tropical:)  The part of the head that is
below that which is called the
قَمَحْدُوَة. (L.) ―
-b6-
قُنْفُذٌ (assumed tropical:)  A place that produces tangled herbage: (S, L,
K:) an elevated place abounding with trees: AHn
says, that what is thus termed is in hard ground,
between what is termed قُفّ and sand. (L.) ―
-b7-
قُنْفُذُ رَمْلٍ (assumed tropical:)  Sand collected together, (Aboo-Kheyreh, L, K,) and elevated, (K,) or somewhat
elevated: (Aboo-Kheyreh, L:) or the abundance
of trees of sand: or, as some say, قُنْفَذُ رَمْلٍ, with
fet-h, signifies abundance and tallness of trees of
sand. (L.) ―
-b8-  قُنْفُذٌ (L, K) and with ة (L) (assumed tropical:)  A
tree in the midst of sand. (L, K.) ―
-b9-  قَنَافِذُ
(assumed tropical:)  Mountains that are not long: or long and
narrow tracts, (أَحْبُل, L, K, TA: in the CK,
أَجْبُل, or mountains,) of sand: (L, K:) or hills
with pointed heads (نَبَكٌ) lying in the way, or
road. (Th, L, K.)
