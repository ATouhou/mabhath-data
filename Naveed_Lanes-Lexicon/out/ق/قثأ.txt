اقثأت الأَرْضُ, (AZ, S, O,) or اقثأ المَكَانُ, (K,)
The land, or the place, abounded with the [species
of cucumber called] قِثّآء. (AZ, S, O, K.) And
اقثأ القَوْمُ The people had abundance of قثّآء. (S,
O, K.)
قِثَّآءٌ (S, O, Msb, K) and قُثَّآءٌ, (O, Msb, K,) the
former of which, with kesr, in the more common,
(Msb, TA,) [A certain vegetable,] well-known:
(K, TA:) [a species of cucumber; cucumis sativus
β fructu flavo majore: (Delile's Floræ Ægypt.
Illustr., no. 928:]) or the [cucumber called] خِيَار
[q. v.]: (S, O, K:) or a general name for the
خِيَار, the عَجُور [q. v.], and the فَقُّوس [or فَقُّوص
q. v.]: but some apply the name to a species resembling the خيار: (Msb, TA:) and it is said
that it is lighter (أَخَفُّ) than the خيار: also that
عَجُور signifies large قِثّآء: (TA:) the n. un. is
قثّآءة. (S, O, Msb.) ―

and مَقْثُؤَةٌ, (Msb,) A place, or land, of قثّآء, (S,
Msb, K,) where قثّآء are sown and grow. (TA.)
