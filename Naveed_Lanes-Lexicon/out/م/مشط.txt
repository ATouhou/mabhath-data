the woman's hair.] (S, TA.) ―

مُشْط. (TA, art. نمص.) ―
-b3-  Also مُشْطٌ (tropical:)  [An
upright loom;] a loom with which one weaves,
set upright: (K, TA:) pl. أَمْشَاطٌ. (TA.) You
say ضَرَبَ النَّاسِجُ بِمِشْطِهِ and أَمْشَاطِهِ (tropical:)  [app.
meaning The weaver wove with his upright loom
and his upright looms]. (TA.) ―
-b4-  (tropical:)  [The metatarsal bones;] the سُلَامَيَات of the upper part of
the foot; (S, K;) i. e. the slender bones spread
upon the foot, exclusive of the toes; [also called,
more particularly, or perhaps only called, مُشْطُ
القَدَمِ or مُشْطُ الرِّجْلِ:] pl. أَمْشَاطٌ. (TA.) You
say, اِنكَسَرَ مُشْطُ قَدَمِهِ (tropical:)  [His metatarsal bones
broke]. (TA.) And قَامُوا عَلَى أَمْشَاطِ أَرْجُلِهِمْ
(tropical:)  [They stood upon their metatarsal bones]. (TA.)
