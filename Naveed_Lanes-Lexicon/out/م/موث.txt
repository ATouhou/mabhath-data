موث1
مَاثَ, aor. يَمُوثُ, inf. n. مَوْثٌ (and in the
CK مَوَثٌ) and مَوَثَانٌ; (S, K;) as also مَاثَ,
aor. يَمِيثُ; (TA;) and ↓ اماث; (Hr;) but this
is disapproved by IAth; He steeped a thing in
water, and mashed it with his hand: (TA:) he
mixed and moistened a thing in water. (S, K.)
[See also art. ميث.] ―
-b2-  See also 7. ―
-b3-  ماثَتِ الأَرْضُ
The land became soft and even. (Msb.)
