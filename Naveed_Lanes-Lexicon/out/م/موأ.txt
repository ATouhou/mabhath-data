موأمآءٌ
Water. ―
-b2-  مَآءُ الظَّهْرِ [The seminal fluid].
(K, voce اِنْبَتَّ.) See Kur, lxxxvi. 6, 7, and
see 4 in art. روق. ―
-b3-  مَآءٌ
Lustre [likened to
water, and running water,] of the teeth, (IbrD,)
&c: see ظَلْمٌ and دَرْزٌ and غَرْبٌ. ―
-b4-  مَآءٌ The water
of a sword: see فِرِنْدٌ and رَوْنَقٌ: also its lustre.
―
-b5-  سَيْفٌ كَثِيرُ المَآءِ [A sword much diversified
with wavy marks or streaks in its grain; as
are the swords of Damascus &c.] (TA voce
إِبْرِيقٌ.) ―
-b6-  بَنَاتُ المَآءِ
i. q.
الغَرَانِيقُ. [storks or
cranes]. (L, art. وضر.) See عِرْسٌ. ―
-b7-  اِبْنُ المَآءِ
is (tropical:)  A kind of bird; pl. بَنَاتُ المَآءِ; (Mgh in art.
بنى:) the aquatic bird; the bird of the water.
(Msb in art. بنو.) ―
-b8-  مَآءُ الشَّبَابِ: see شَبَابٌ. ―
-b9-
مَآءُ الوَجْهِ: see حَقَنَ and تَشَهَّلَ. ―
-b10-  مَآءُ الذَّهَبِ
Gold-wash for gilding: and مَآءُ الفِضَّةِ
Silverwash for silvering: you say, طَلَاهُ بِمَآءِ الذَّهَبِ,
and الفِضَّةِ
He washed it over with gold, and
silver. ―
-b11-  مَآءٌ is used as a coll. gen. n., of which
the n. un. is مَاءَةٌ; and therefore is sometimes
made, as a pl., to have a pl. epithet: ex. مَاءٌ
عِذَابٌ. (See عَذْبٌ). مَآءَةٌ signifies A water;
or some water.
