رشأ1
رَشَأَ i. q. جَامَعَ [Inivit feminam]. (K.)
-A2-
رَشَأَتْ She (a gazelle) brought forth. (K.)
رَشَأٌ A young gazelle, (S, Msb, K,) that has
become active, or in motion, (S, Msb,) or that
has become strong, (K,) and has walked (S, Msb,
K) with its mother: (K:) pl. أَرْشَآءٌ. (Msb, K.)
[In the following saying, I find it written as
though with medd; app. for the sake of assimilation to النِّسَآء:] عِنْدِى جَارِيَةٌ مِنَ النِّسَآءِ أَشْبَهُ شَىْء ٍ
بِالرَّشَآءِ, meaning [I have with me a young woman
most like to] the [young] gazelle: so in the A.
(TA.)
