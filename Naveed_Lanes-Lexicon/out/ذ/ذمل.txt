ذمل1
ذَمَلَ, (S, M, K,) or ذَمَلَتْ, (T,) aor.
ذَمُلَ (T,
S, M, K) and
ذَمِلَ, (S, M, K,) inf. n. ذَمِيلٌ (T, S,
M, K) and ذَمْلٌ and ذُمُولٌ and ذَمَلَانٌ, (M, K,)
said of a camel, (T, S,) He, or she, went a gentle
pace: (T, M, K:) or went a pace above that
which is termed العَنَقُ, (S, M, K,) and above that
which is termed التَّزَيُّدُ: [for] A'Obeyd says,
when the pace rises a little above that which is
termed العَنَقُ, it is termed التَّزَيُّدُ; and when it
rises above this, it is termed الذَّمِيلُ; and then,
الرَّسِيمُ: As says that no camel goes the pace
termed الذَّمِيل for a day and a night except the
مَهْرِى. (S.)
2
ذَمَّلْتُهُ, inf. n. تَذْمِيلٌ, I urged him, or made
him, (namely, a camel, TA,) to go the pace above
mentioned. (K.)
نَاقَةٌ ذَمُولٌ [A she-camel that is accustomed to
go the pace above mentioned]: (M, K:) pl. ذُمُلٌ,
(M, and so in my MS. copy of the K,) or ذُمْلٌ.
(TA: in the CK ذُمَّل.)
ذَمِيلَةٌ Fatigued, or jaded; (IAar, T, K;) applied to a she-camel. (IAar, TA.)
نَاقَةٌ ذَامِلَةٌ [A she-camel going the pace above
mentioned]: pl. ذَوَامِلُ. (T.)
