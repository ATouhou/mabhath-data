ذيم1
ذَامَهُ, (T, M, Msb, K,) first pers. ذِمْتُهُ, (S,)
aor. يَذِيمُ, (T, &c.,) inf. n. ذَيْمٌ and ذَامٌ, (S, M,
Msb, K,) He blamed, or found fault with, him,
or it, (T, S, M, Msb, K,) namely, a commodity;
like ذَأَمَهُ: (Msb:) accord. to Akh, ذِمْتُهُ and
ذَأَمْتُهُ and ذَمَمْتُهُ all signify the same. (S.)
ذَامٌ: see what next follows, in two places.
ذَيْمٌ and ↓ ذَامٌ are inf. ns., (S, M, Msb, K,) and
are syn. with عَيْبٌ [as such, and also as meaning
A vice, fault, defect, or the like; in the latter
sense syn. with ذَامٌّ, which belongs to art. ذم]:
(T, S, M, Msb, * K:) or, as some say, syn. with
ذَمٌّ [blame, &c.]. (M.) It is said in a prov.,
↓ لَاتَعْدَمُ الحَسْنَآءُ ذَامًا [The beautiful female is not
without a defect]. (S.)
مَذِيمٌ Blamed, or found fault with; (S, Msb,
K;) as also ↓ مَذْيُومٌ: (S, K:) the former defective,
and the latter complete: (S:) applied [app. to a
man; (see the dial. var. مَذْؤُومٌ, as used in the
Kur vii. 17;) and] to a commodity. (Msb.)
مَذْيُومٌ: see what next precedes.
