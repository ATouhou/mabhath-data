-b5-  He was disdainful,
or averse [from a person or thing]; or he disdained, or scorned: (K:) he became affected

ذَئِرُوا لِقَتْلَى عَامِرٍ وَتَغَضَّبُوا

disapprobation on account of the slain of 'Ámir

(S, K *) Verily thy tears are accompanied by a

ذِئَارٌ Fresh camels' or similar dung, (بَعَر,)
mixed with dust, or earth, with which a she-camel's teats are smeared, that she may not be
sucked. (M, * K, * TA.) [See also art. ذير.]
ذَائِرٌ Angry; (IAar, T, K;) as also ↓ ذَئِرٌ.
(K.) ―
-b2-  Contracted in the bosom, and evil in
disposition. (Ibn-Es-Seed.) ―
-b3-  Disdaining, or
averse [from a person or thing]: disdainful;
scornful. (IAar, T.) ―
-b4-  A woman disobedient to
her husband, and hating him; (S, K;) averse
from him; and emboldened against him; (S;)
as also ↓ ذَئِرٌ, and ↓ مُذَائِرٌ: (K:) [all without ذَائِرٌ:]
and in a similar sense ذَائِرٌ is applied to a man.
(S, * TA.)
مُذَائِرٌ: see ذَائِرٌ. ―
-b2-  Also, [without ة,] A she-camel averse from her young one when she has

true love: (S, M, K:) or evil in disposition. (M.)
