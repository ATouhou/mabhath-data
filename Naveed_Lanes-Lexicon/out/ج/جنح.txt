man: (Msb:) and also the يد [i. e. arm, sometimes also meaning hand, (see a signification of
جَنَحَ,)] of a man: (L, TA:) and (K) the upper
arm, or arm from the shoulder to the elbow:
(Zj, L, K:) each of these is so called because it
is on one side of the body: (L:) and the armpit: (K:) pl. أَجْنِحَةٌ (S, Msb, K) and أَجْنُحٌ:

جَانِحَةٌ sing. of جَوَانِحُ; (S, K;) which latter
signifies The ribs of the breast: (A:) or the ribs
that are beneath [those called] the تَرَائِب, of the

the part next the back: (S:) or the anterior

horse and the like, the ribs against which lies the
shoulder-blade: and of a man, the ribs of the
back which are called دَأْى, six in number, three
on the right and three on the left. (L.)
