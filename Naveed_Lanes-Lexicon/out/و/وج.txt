وجوجٌّ A kind of medicine; (S, K;) certain
twigs, or rods, with which one fumigates; (TA;)
or which are used medicinally or remedially;
(T;) [acorum, (Golius,) or acorus; sweet-cane,
or, as some say, galangal: see Diosc., I. i.,
c. 2., referred to by Golius:] thought by ElJawáleekee to be not pure Arabic; i. e., an
arabicized word, from the Persian [وَجْ]: (TA:)
so says J. (S.)
