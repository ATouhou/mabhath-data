وحR. Q. 1
وَحْوَحَ, inf. n. وَحْوَحَةٌ, He uttered a
sound accompanied by a hoarseness, roughness,
harshness, or gruffness, of the voice: (S, K:)
he made his breath to reciprocate in his throat,
so as to be audible, by reason of cold; (TA:)
he blew upon his hand by reason of intense cold.
(S, K.)
وَحْوَحٌ A strong man, (L, K,) who breathes
hard at his work by reason of his briskness and
vigour; (L;) as also ↓ وَحْوَاحٌ. (K.) ―
-b2-  Also
the former, (L, K,) and the latter, (L,) A quick,
or prompt, and sharp-spirited, man. (L, K.) ―
-b3-
Also the former, (S, K,) and the latter, (S,) A
light, or an active, or agile, man. (As, S, K.)
―
-b4-  Also both words, A lord, or chief: pl.
وَحَاوِحُ and وَحَاوِحَةٌ. (L.) ―
-b5-  Also both words,
A barking, or howling, dog. (L, K.)
وَحْوَاحٌ: see what precedes.
