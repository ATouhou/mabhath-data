وهج1
وَهَجَتِ النَّارُ, aor. تَهِجُ, inf. n. وَهْجٌ and
وَهَجَانٌ; (S, L, K;) and وَهَجَتَ, [app. a mistake for وَهِجَت,] aor. تَوْهَجُ, [inf. n. وَهَجٌ;]
(L;) The fire burned; syn. اِتَّقَدَت; (S, K, &c.;)
and so ↓ تَوهّجت; (L;) syn. توقّدت; (S, L, K;)
[or this last has an intensive sense, and signifies,
as does the first accord. to the A, it burned
fiercely; glowed]. ―
-b2-  Also, all these verbs, with
the same inf. ns., It (the sun) burned, or was
hot: and it (a fire) burned from a distance.
(L.) ―
-b3-  Also, all the above verbs, with the
same inf. ns., (tropical:)  It (a day, and a night,) was
violently hot. (L.)
