وذح1
وَذِحَتِ الشَّاتُ, aor. تَوْذَحُ and تَيْذَحُ (S, K)
or تِيذَح, (TA,) inf. n. وَذَحٌ, (S,) The sheep was,
or became, foul with وَذَح [q. v. infrà.] (S, K.)
―
-b2-  وَذِحَ, inf. n. as above, He suffered from an
inflammation and excoriation of the inner sides
of his thighs; (En-Nadr, L, K; *) as also مَذِحَ.
(En-Nadr, L.)
وَذَحٌ The dung and urine that have clung to
the wool of sheep, (K,) or to their tails and the
inner sides of the roots of their thighs (أَرْفَاغها),
and dry [thereon]: (S:) or the filth that clings
to the rump, or buttocks, of the ram: (Th:)
n. un. with ة: pl. وُذْحٌ; like بُدْنٌ, (S, K,) pl. of
بَدَنَةٌ. (S.)
