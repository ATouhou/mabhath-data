ضِئْضِئٌ (S, O, K) and ↓ ضِئْضِىْءٌ and ↓ ضُؤْضُؤٌ
and ↓ ضُوْضُوْءٌ (O, K) and accord. to ISd ↓ ضِئْضَأٌ,
which is of an extr. measure, (TA,) Origin, root,
race, or stock; syn. أَصْلٌ: (S, O, K:) and the
place in which a thing originates; syn. مَعْدِنٌ:
(K:) hence, in a خُطْبَة of Aboo-Tálib, الحَمْدُ لِلّٰهِ
الَّذِى جَعَلَنَا مِنْ ذُرِّيَّةِ إِبْرَاهِيمَ وَزَرْعِ إِسْمَاعِيلَ وَضِئْضِئِ
مَعَدٍّ وَعُنْصُرِ مُضَرَ i. e. [Praise be to God, who
made us to be of the offspring of Abraham and of
the seed of Ishmael and] of the stock of Ma'add
[and of the race of Mudar]: and يَخْرُجُ مِنْ
ضِئْضِئِ هٰذَا, occurring in a trad., i. e. There shall
spring from the stock, or race, of this; or, as some
relate it, it is [صِئْصِئ,] with the unpointed ص,
which means the same: and you say ضِئْضِئُ صِدْقٍ
and صِدْقٍ ↓ ضُؤْضُؤُ [An excellent origin or race]:
(TA:) [see an ex. voce بُؤْبُؤٌ, in art. بأ: and see also
ضِنْءٌ:] or ضِئْضِئٌ &c. signify multitude, and abundance, of offspring or breed; (K, TA;) whence
the ضِئْضِئ of sheep. (TA.)
