عطدعَطْدٌ Hardness, severity, rigour, or difficulty.
(IDrd, * O, * L.)
عَطَوَّدٌ Hard, severe, rigorous, or difficult:
(IDrd, O, L, K:) applied in this sense to anything: and particularly to a journey: or in this
case meaning distant. (L.) ―
-b2-  A quick pace, or
rate of going: (S, O, L, K:) and so عَطَرَّدٌ [q. v.].
(L.) ―
-b3-  A high mountain: (O, L, K, TA: [in
the CK, من الحِبالِ is erroneously put for مِنَ
الجِبَالِ:]) as also عَصَوَّدٌ and عَطَرَّدٌ. (L.) ―
-b4-  A
long day: (O, K:) a complete day (L) or year:
(Ibn-'Abbád, O, K:) a whole day. (T, O, L, K.)
One says, ذَهَبَ عَطَوَّدًا He went away a whole
day. (O, K.) ―
-b5-  A conspicuous, clear, open,
road, along which one goes whithersoever he will.
(ISh, O, L, K.) ―
-b6-  A generous, noble, liberal,
man. (Ibn-'Abbád, O, K.) ―
-b7-  A sharpened spearhead. (Ibn-'Abbád, O, K.) ―
-b8-  Accord. to [IDrd
and] Az [and J], this word is quasi-coordinate
to the quinqueliteral-radical class. (TA.)
