تَعَيَّسَتِ الإِبِلُ The camels were, or became,
[of the colour termed عَيَسٌ or] white inclining to
black. (O, K.)
عَيَسٌ (S, TA) and ↓ عِيسَةٌ, the latter of the
measure فُعْلَةٌ, [originally عُيْسَةٌ.] like صُهْبَةٌ and
كُمْتَةٌ, (Lth, O, TA,) Whiteness in a camel, mixed
with somewhat of] the red hue termed] شُقْرَة; (S,
TA;) [i. e., a reddish whiteness:] or [a dingy
whiteness;] whiteness in which is a mixture of
clearness with slight darkness: (TA:) [or a yel-lowish whiteness see أَعْيَسُ.]
عِيسَةٌ: see the next preceding paragraph.
أَعْيَسُ A camel of a white colour mixed with
somewhat of [the red hue termed] شُقْرَة; (S, O,
K; *) [i. e., reddish white] or [dingy white;]
white with a slight darkness (Msb:) or inclining
to yellow: [i. e., yellowish white:] (I Aar:) fem.
عَيْسَآءُ: pl. عِيسٌ: (S, O, Msb, K:) the camels thus
termed are said to be of good breed. (S, O,) Also
A gazelle, or an antelope, and a bull, [app. meaning a wild bull,] in which is [a hue such as is
termed] أَدْمَة. (TA.) And you say رَجُلٌ أَعْيَسُ
الشَّعَرِ A man having white hair. (TA.) And
رَسْمٌ أَعْيَسُ A white mark, trace, relic, or remain.
