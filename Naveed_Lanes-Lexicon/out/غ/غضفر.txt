غضفرQ. 1
غَضْفَرَ He (a jade, or hackney, TA) was,
or became, heavy, or sluggish. (K, TA.)
غَضْفَرٌ: see the next paragraph; the former,
in two places.
غُضَافِرٌ: see the next paragraph; the former,
in two places.
غَضَنْفَرٌ Big, thick, or rude, in body or person;
(S, K;) as also ↓ غَضْفَرٌ, which is the original
form; the ن in the former being augmentative:
(Az, TA:) or simply, big, thick, or rude: applied
to a man: (Lth, TA:) or rude, or coarse, [in disposition or in make,] and big, or thick: (TA:)
as also ↓ غَنَضْفَرٌ and ↓ غَضْفَرٌ; (K;) applied to a
man: (TA:) or big, or thick, (AA, TA.) in
make, (TA,) and wrinkled; (AA, TA;) applied
to a lion. (TA.) You say also أُذُنٌ غَضَنْفَرَةٌ,
meaning, A big, or thick, fleshy, ear. (AO, L.)
―
-b2-  [Hence,] الغَضَنْفَرُ The lion; (Lth, S, K;) as
also ↓ الغُضَافِرُ. (TS, K.)
غَضَنْفَرٌ: see the next preceding paragraph.
