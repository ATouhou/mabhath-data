خِبَآءٌ A kind of structure; (K;) [i. e.] one of
the بُيُوت [or kinds of tents] of the Arabs, (IAth,
TA,) peculiarly of wool, (Yaakoob, Th,) or of
camels' fur, or of wool, (IAar, S, IAth, K,) or
of [goats'] hair, (K,) or not of [goats'] hair,
(S,) [except in cold countries and in fertile
regions, where the goats have abundant hair,
for the goats of the Arabs of the desert have
short hair, not long enough to be spun, (see
بَيْتٌ,)] smaller than the مِظَلَّة, (IAar,) upon
two poles, or three; what is above this kind
being termed بَيْتٌ; (S;) or a tent having one
pole; that which has more than one pole being
termed بَيْتٌ: (AZ, TA in art. ربع:) pl. أَخْبِيَةٌ,
(S, TA,) without ء: (TA:) most of the lexicologists hold that its radical letters are خبى. (TA
in art. خبأ, q. v.) ―
