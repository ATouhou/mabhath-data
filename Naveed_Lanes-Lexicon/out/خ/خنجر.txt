خنجرخَنْجَرٌ (S, Mgh, Msb, K) and خِنْجِرٌ (Msb) and
خِنْجَرٌ, (K,) the last of a rare form, like دِرْهَمٌ,
(TA,) A knife: or a great knife: (K:) or a
kind of large knife [or dagger, generally curved,
and double-edged], (S, Mgh, Msb,) called in
Persian دَشْنَهْ: (Mgh:) pl. خَنَاجِرُ. (Msb.) Some
say, [as, for instance, the author of the Msb,]
that the ن is augmentative, the measure being
فنعل. (TA.)
-A2-  Also the first, (K,) and ↓ خُنْجُورٌ,
(As, S, TA,) or ↓ خُنْجُورَةٌ, and ↓ خَنْجَرَةٌ, (K,) A
she-camel abounding with milk: (As, S, K:) pl.
خَنَاجِرُ. (S.) And ↓ خُنْجُورَةٌ A bulky she-camel.
(K.)
خَنْجَرَةٌ: see above.
خُنْجُورٌ and خُنْجُورَةٌ: see خَنْجَرٌ, in three
places.
