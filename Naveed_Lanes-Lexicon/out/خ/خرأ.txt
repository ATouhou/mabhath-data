خرأ1
خَرِئَ, aor.
خَرَاَ, (S, Mgh, Msb, K,) inf. n.
خَرَآءَةٌ (S, Mgh, K) and ↓ خِرَآءَةٌ, (K,) which is
the more chaste form, (O,) or this may be a
quasi-inf. n., (El-Khattábee, Msb,) and, accord.
to some, ↓ خِرَآءٌ, (MF,) or this is a quasi-inf. n.,
(Lth, * Msb, K, * MF,) like صِيَامٌ for صَوْمٌ, or, as
some say, a pl. of خَرْءٌ, (Msb, MF,) and خَرْءٌ also
is an inf. n., (S, K,) and خُرُوْءٌ, (K, TA,) like
قُعُودٌ, (TA,) or خُرُوْءَةٌ, (so in a MS. copy of the
K and in the CK,) He voided his excrement, or
ordure: (S, Mgh, Msb, K:) or خِرَآءَةٌ signifies
the retiring alone, and sitting down, to satisfy a
want [of nature]. (IAth.)
خَرْءٌ: see what next follows.
خُرْءٌ Human excrement, or ordure: (S, Mgh, *
Msb, K;) as also ↓ خَرْءٌ: (Msb, TA:) and sometimes applied to the dung of the dog, and of the
field-rat, and of the ant, and of the fly: (TA:)
pl. خُرُوْءٌ, (S, Mgh, Msb, K,) which is also a pl.
of خَرْءٌ, (Msb, TA,) and خُرْآنٌ, (K,) which is
anomalous, and خُرُؤٌ; (TA;) and some say that
خِرَآءٌ is a pl. of خَرْءٌ. (Msb, MF.)
خِرَآءٌ: see 1.
خِرَآءَةٌ: see 1.
خَارِئٌ act. part. n. of 1. (TA.)
مَخْرَأَةٌ A place where excrement, or ordure, is
voided; [a privy;] (S, K;) as also مَخْرَاةٌ, (K,)
and ↓ مَخْرُؤَةٌ; (T, S, K, TA;) the last written in
some copies of the K مَخْرِئَةٌ; and in one, مِخْرَأَةٌ.
(TA.)
مَخْرُؤَةٌ: see what next precedes.
