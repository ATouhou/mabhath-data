خزفخَزَفٌ Pottery; jars; or earthern vessels; syn.
جَرٌّ; (Lth, S, K; [and so in the present day;])
and anything made of clay, and baked, so that
it becomes فَخَّار: (IDrd, K:) or clay made into
vessels, before it is baked; i. q. صَلْصَالٌ: when
baked, it is called فَخَّار. (Msb.) [See an ex. in a
verse cited voce إِنْ, p. 107.]
خَزَفِىٌّ [Of, or relating to, pottery, or jars,
&c.;] rel. n. of خَزَفٌ. (TA.) ―
