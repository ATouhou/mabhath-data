خزعبلخَزَعْبَلٌ Stories that are held to be clever, ingenious, or elegant, (IDrd, K,) and at which one
laughs. (IDrd.)
خُزَعْبَلٌ i. q. بَاطِلٌ [app. as a subst., meaning A
false, or vain, saying or deed or affair or thing]; as
also ↓ خُزَعْبِيلٌ: (K:) or, accord. to El-Jarmee, (S,
TA,) the latter, (S,) or each, (TA,) false, or vain,
sayings or deeds or affairs or things. (S, TA.)
خُزَعْبَلَةٌ or خُزَعْبِلَةٌ (accord. to different copies of
the K) A wonderful thing. (IAar, K.) And
[the pls.] خُزَعْبِلَاتٌ and خُزَعْبَلَاتٌ False, or vain,
stories. (Har p. 16.)
خُزَعْبِيلٌ: see خُزَعْبَلٌ.
خُزَعْبِيلَةٌ A laughable thing; a thing that makes
people laugh. (S, K) One says, هَاتِ بَعْضَ
خُزَعْبِيلَاتِكَ [Give me some of thy laughable thingsor stories]. (S.)
