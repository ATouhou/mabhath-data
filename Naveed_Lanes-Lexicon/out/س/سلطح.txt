سلطحQ. 3
اِسْلَنْطَحَ It (a thing) was, or became, long
and wide. (AA, O and L in this art.: mentioned
in the S in art. سطح.) ―
-b2-  It (a valley) became
wide: (K:) accord. to IF, both the ل and ن are
added to give intensiveness to the signification.
(O.) ―
-b3-  He (a man) extended himself or became
extended [app. on the ground]; syn. اِنْبَسَطَ: (L:)
or he became thrown down upon his face: or he
lay, or lay as though thrown down or extended:
or he stretched himself; or lay, and stretched
himself; upon his face, extended upon the ground:
syn. اِنْطَبَحَ: (O:) or he lay as though thrown
down or extended, upon the back of his neck:
(Ibn-'Abbád, O:) or he (a man, L) fell upon his
face: (L, K:) and upon his back. (L.)
سُلْطَحٌ, (K,) or ↓ سُلْطُوحٌ, (O,) A smooth
mountain: (O, K:) so says Ibn-'Abbád. (O.)
سَلْطَحَةٌ, applied to a girl, or young woman.
Broad. (K.)
سُلْطُوحٌ: see سُلْطُحٌ.
سُلَاطِحٌ wide. (Lth, T, O, K.) It is said of
rain [as meaning Wide-spreading]. (O.)
سَلَنْطَحٌ A wide open tract; (IDrd, O, K;) as
also ↓ مُسْلَنْطِحٌ. (K.)
مُسْلَنْطِحٌ: see what next precedes.
