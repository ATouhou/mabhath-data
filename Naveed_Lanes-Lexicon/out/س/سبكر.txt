سبكرQ. 4
اِسْبَكَرَّ i. q. اِسْبَطَرَّ in its several significations: (K:) i. e. (TA) ―
-b2-  He stretched himself:
(TA:) he lay upon his side, and stretched himself: (AA, S, TA:) [&c.] ―
-b3-  He (a youth, or
young man,) became tall: (Lh:) and اسبكرّت
She (a girl) became of erect and justly-proportioned stature. (S, K.) An ex. occurs in a verse
of Imra-el-Keys, cited voce مِجْوَلٌ. (S.) ―
-b4-  It
(a plant) became tall and full-grown: (TA:) and
it (anything) became extended, and long, or tall.
(Lh.) ―
-b5-  He went at random, or heedlessly;
without consideration, or certain aim. (Lh.) ―
-b6-
It (a river) flowed. (TA.) ―
-b7-  اسبكرّت عَيْنُهُ His
eye shed tears. (Lh.) But ISd says that this is
not known in classical Arabic. (TA.)
مُسْبَكِرٌّ [act. part. n. of the verb above]. ―

-b3-
