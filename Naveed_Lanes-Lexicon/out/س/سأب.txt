سَأْبٌ and ↓ مِسْأَبٌ A [skin such as is termed] زِقّ,
(S, M, K,) for wine: (M:) or such as is large:
(M, K:) or a زِقّ of any kind: (M:) or a receptacle of skin, or leather, in which the زِقّ is put:
(M, K:) the former also occurs in a verse in
which it is read without ء, for the sake of the
rhyme: (M:) and its pl. is سُؤُوبٌ: (S, M, K:)
and (so in the S, but in the K “ or,”) the latter
(مسأب) signifies a skin for honey; (S, K;) and
in a verse of Aboo-Dhu-eyb, (S, M, K,) cited
voce خَافَةٌ, (S, M,) it is written مِسَابٌ, (S, M, K,)
without ء: (S, M:) and it signifies also a skin in
which clarified butter is put. (S and L voce
مِسَادٌ.)
