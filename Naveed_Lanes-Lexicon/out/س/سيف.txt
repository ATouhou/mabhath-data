سَيْفٌ A sword; (MA, PS, &c.;) a certain
thing with which one smites; (M;) well known:

added that its author has mentioned these names
in [his book entitled] الرَّوْضُ المَسْلُوفُ:) [for the
names of particular parts thereof, see ذُبَابٌ:] pl.
[of pauc.] أَسْيَافٌ (S, M, O, Msb, K) and أَسْيُفٌ

Msb, K) and [quasi-pl. n.] ↓ مَسْيَفَةٌ, like مَشْيَخَةٌ,
(O, K, TA,) or مَسِيفَةٌ, like مَشِيخَةٌ. (CK.)

سِيفٌ The shore (سَاحِل) of the sea or of a great
river: (S, M, O, Mgh, Msb, K:) and the side (سَاحِل)
of a valley: or [the margin of the shore of a sea
or of a great river; for it is added,] every سَاحِل
has a سِيف: or السِّيفُ is applied only to the سِيف
[or sea-shore, or seaboard,] of 'Omán: (K:) [if
otherwise applied,] its pl. is أَسْيَافٌ. (S, M.) One
says, هُمْ أَهْلُ أَسْيَاف ٍ وَأَرْيَاف ٍ [They are people of
the shores of the sea or of a great river, and of
the tracts of towns, or villages, and cultivated
lands]. (TA.)
