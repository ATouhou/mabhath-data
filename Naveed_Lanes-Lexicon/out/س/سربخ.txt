سربخQ. 1
سَرْبَخْتُ فِى المَشْىِ, inf. n. سَرْبَخَةٌ, I walked, or marched, an hour, or a while: (JK:)
[or in the middle of the day: and gently, or in a
leisurely manner: for] ―
-b2-  سَرْبَخَةٌ signifies The
walking, or marching, in the middle of the day.
(K.) And The walking, or marching, gently, or
in a leisurely manner. (JK, K.) ―
-b3-  And The
being light, or agile, or active; light in any work
or action, or unsteady or lightwitted, or so on an
occasion of anger, or hasty, with foolishness or
ignorance; syn. خِفَّةٌ and نَزَقٌ. (K.)
سَرْبَخٌ A wide, or spacious, land: (S, K:) or
one far extending: (TA:) and a land in which
one cannot find his way. (K, * TA.)
مَهْمَهُ سِرْبَاخٌ A desert, or waterless desert, wide,
or spacious, (K, TA,) in the sides: (TA:) and
↓ مَهْمَهٌ مُسَرْبَخٌ a desert, or waterless desert, far
extending, (K, * TA,) wide, or spacious: so in a
verse of Aboo-Duwád cited voce مَرْدُونٌ, q. v.
(TA.)
مُسَرْبَخٌ: see what next precedes.
