سمحقسِمْحَاقٌ [The pericranium; i. e.] the thin skin,
(T, Mgh, Msb,) or thin integument, (S, Mgh, K,)
above the skull: (T, S, Mgh, Msb, K:) and any
thin skin resembling that; (Msb;) or [the periosteum of any bone; i. e.] the skin that is between
the bone and the flesh, above the bone and beneath
the flesh; every bone having what is thus termed:
(TA:) [pl. سَمَاحِيقُ. ―
-b2-  Hence,] one says, عَلَى
ثَرْبِ الشَّاةِ سَمَاحِيقُ مِنْ شَحْمٍ, (S, K, TA,) i. e.
(tropical:)  [Upon the fat that covers the stomach and
bowels of the sheep or goat is or are] a thin
integument [or thin integuments of fat]. (TA.)
―
-b3-  And سَمَاحِيقُ السَّمَآءِ (tropical:)  The [strata or] thin portions of cloud. (S, K, TA.) ―
-b4-  سِمْحَاقٌ also signifies [The cicatrix which is] the mark of circumcision. (TA.) ―
-b5-  And A wound by which
the head is broken (شَجَّةٌ [q. v.]) reaching to the
thin skin, or integument, thus called. (T, S,
Msb, K.)
سُكْحُوقٌ Tall; applied to a palm-tree; (S, O,
K;) as also سَحُوقٌ: (S:) or tall and thin: (Lth,
TA:) not known to Az on any other authority
than that of Lth. (TA.)
[J held the م in each of these words, as he
says, to be augmentative; and has therefore mentioned them in art. سحق.]
