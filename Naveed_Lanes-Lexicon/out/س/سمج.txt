سمّجهُ, (A, O, L, Msb, K,) inf. n. تَسْمِيجٌ,
(O, K,) He, or it, rendered it foul, unseemly, or
ugly; (O, Msb, * K;) or devoid of beauty. (L,
Msb.) One says, مَا سَمَّجَهُ عِنْدِى إِلَّا كَذَا [Nothing rendered it foul, unseemly, &c., in my estimation, but such a thing]. (A.)

استسمجهُ He reckoned it, or esteemed it,
foul, unseemly, or ugly; (S, O;) or devoid of
beauty. (L.) One says, أَنَا أَسْتَسْمِجُ فِعْلَكَ [I
reckon, or esteem, thy deed foul, or unseemly].
(A, TA.)
