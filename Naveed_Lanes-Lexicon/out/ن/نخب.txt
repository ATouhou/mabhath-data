نَخِبٌ (S, K) and ↓ نَخْبٌ and ↓ نَخْبَةٌ (so accord.
to the TA: in the CK نُخْبَةٌ:) and ↓ نُخْبَةٌ (in

and ↓ نَخِيبٌ and ↓ مَنْخُوبٌ and ↓ مُنْتَخَبٌ (S, K)
and ↓ يَنْخُوبٌ, (K,) to which the CK adds ↓ نَخَبٌ,
A cowardly man; a coward; as though his
heart were drawn out; (S;) i. e., having no heart.
(TA.) ↓ قَلْبٌ نَخِيبٌ [A cowardly heart]: نخيب
signifies a coward, who has no heart: or, accord.
to some, one who acts corruptly. (TA.) Pl. (of
↓ نخيب, TA,) نُخُبٌ: (K:) of ↓ منخوب, مَنْخُوبُونَ,
and sometimes, in poetry, accord. to IAth,
مَنَاخِبُ: and Aboo-Bekr mentions نخبات as a
pl. of نخبة. (TA.)
نَخْبَةٌ: see نَخْبٌ and نَخِبٌ.
