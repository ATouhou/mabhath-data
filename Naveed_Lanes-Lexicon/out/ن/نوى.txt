نَوًى
Date-stones: they are often used as food
for camels; (see إِبِلٌ نَوَوِيَّةٌ;) and for this purpose are bruised, and sometimes mixed with
barley, and then moistened: see بَسِيسَةٌ. ―
-b2-  نَوَاةٌ
What is cut off in the circumcision of a girl.
(Lh, in TA, voce عُذْرَةٌ.) The name shows
that this is the prepuce of the clitoris, the end
of which resembles the end of a date-stone.
But see بَظْرٌ. ―

-b4-  نَوًى

(TA in art. جب.) ―
-b5-  نَوًى The tract, or region
towards which one goes (S) in journeying, whether

conduct: (TA:) see an ex. voce صَرْفٌ (third
sentence), and عَقَرَ. It is of the fem. gender.
