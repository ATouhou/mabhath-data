نَشَحَ, aor.
نَسَحَ, inf. n. نَشْحٌ and نُشُوحٌ, He
drank a little, (L,) less than what would satisfy
him: (S, L, K:) or, contr., he drank until he
was full: (L, K:) as also ↓ انتشح. (L.) ―
-b2-
نَشَحَ He gave his camel a little water to drink:
(L:) he watered horses so as to allay the vehemence of their thirst. (T, L, K.)
8
إِنْتَسَحَ see 1.
نَشُوحٌ A little water. (S, K.) Abu-n-Nejm
says, describing asses,
حَتَّى إِذَا مَا غَيَّبَتْ نَشُوحَا
(S, L,) meaning Until, when they had taken into
their bellies a little water: or نشوح signifies a
drink less than suffices to satisfy. (L.)
