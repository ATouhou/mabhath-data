نأR. Q. 1
نَأْنَأَهُ He fed him, or nourished him,
well. (K.)
-A2-  He restrained him, or turned him
back, (El-Umawee, S, K,) from a thing that he
desired to do. (El-Umawee, S.)
-A3-  نَأْنَأَ فِى الرَّأْىِ,
inf. n. نَأْنَأَةٌ and مُنَأْنَأَةٌ; [the latter an unusual form
of inf. n.;] and ↓ تَنَأْنَأَ; He was weak, (M, K,)
or confused; (S;) and not firm or sound, (S, M,
K,) in his judgment, or opinion. (S, M, K.) ―
-b2-
نَأْنَأَ فِى الأَمْرِ He was weak in the affair. (S.) ―
-b3-
نَأْنَأَ عَنْهُ, and ↓ تَنَأْنَأَ, He was unable to do it. (K.)
―
-b4-  نَأْنَأَ, inf. n. نَأْنَأَةٌ; (AA;) and ↓ تَنَأْنَأَ; (S;)
He was weak, feeble, or remiss. (AA, S, TA.)
R. Q. 2
see R. Q. 1 in three places.
نَأْنَأٌ and ↓ نَأْنَآءٌ (S, K) and ↓ نُؤْنُوءٌ and ↓ مُنَأْنَأٌ
Weak; cowardly. (S, K.) ―
-b2-  نَأْنَأٌ One who frequently turns about, or rolls, the pupil, or black
part, of his eye. (K.)
نَأْنَأَةٌ: see the verb. ―
-b2-  Weakness. (AA, S.)
―
-b3-  طُوبَى لِمَنْ مَاتَ فِى النّأنأةِ [Good betide him
who hath died in (the time of) weakness!] i. e., in
the first of El-Islám, before it acquired strength,
(S,) and its adherents and assistants multiplied.
(TA.)
نَأْنَآءٌ, نُؤْنُوءٌ, and مُنَأْنَأٌ: see نَأْنَأٌ.
