نرزنَوْرُوزٌ: see what follows.
نَيْرُوزٌ, an arabicized word, (S, A, Msb,) from
نَوْرُوزْ, (A, K,) which in Persian; meaning “ new
day; ” (TA;) and ↓ نَوْرُوزٌ; but the former,
which is of the measure فَيْعُولٌ, is the better in
repute, because فَوْعُولٌ is not the measure of an
Arabic word; (Msb;) The first day of the year;
New-year's-day: (A, Msb, K:) with the Persians,
when the sun enters Aries: and with the Copts,
the first of [the month] Toot [the ancient Thoth,
or the tenth of September, N. S., excepting when
immediately following their leap-year, which is
when our next ensuing year is a leap-year].
(Msb, TA.) The word نيروز is said to have
been first used in the time of the 'Abbásee Khaleefehs; but it is related to have been used in
the time of 'Alee. (TA.)
