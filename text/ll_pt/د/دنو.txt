                  <i>Nearer,</i> and <i>nearest;</i> opposed to اَقْصَى: (TA:) fem. دُنْيَا; (M, TA;) in which the [radical] و is changed into ى, as in عُلْيَا and قُصْيَا: (ISd, TA voce بُقْوَى:) [the pl. of the masc. is اَدَانٍ and اَدْنَوْنَ; the latter in the accus. and gen. اَدْنَيْنِ: and] the pl. of the fem., دُنًى, (S, K, TA,) like كُبَرٌ pl. of كُبْرَى, and صُغَرٌ pl. of صُغْرَى; (S, TA;) said by some to be extr. and strange [in respect of usage]; and El-Mutanebbee has been blamed for using it; (MF, TA;) but in the case referred to he has used الدُّنَى for الدُّنْيَا, [not as a pl.,] suppressing the ى by poetic license. (TA.) [Hence,] غُلِبَتِ الرُّومُ فِى اَدْنَى <br>\
                  الاَرْضِ, in the Kur xxx. 1 and 2, <i>The Greeks have been overcome in the nearer,</i> or <i>nearest, part of the land.</i> (Bd, Jel.) And الجَمْرَةُ الدُّنْيَا [<i>The nearest heap of pebbles;</i>] <i>the heap of pebbles nearest to Minè.</i> (TA. [See art. جمر.]) And  <br>\
