                  رَايِب: (M, K:) or <i>a skin that is wrapped up</i> [in order that its milk may thicken more quickly by its being kept warm] <i>until it attains the fit time for the churning.</i> (S.) It is said in a prov., اَهْوَنُ مَظْلُومٍ سِقَا=ءٌ مُرَوَّبٌ, (T, S, M, A,) meaning [<i>The lightest in estimation</i>] <i>of what is drunk,</i> or <i>given to be drunk,</i> [of milk,] <i>before its butter comes forth from it</i> (As, T) [<i>is that in</i>] <i>a skin that is wrapped up</i> &amp;amp;c., as expl. above: (S:) [or (assumed tropical:)  <i>the least to be esteemed of the wronged is he who remains quiet,</i> or <i>inert, like milk not yet in a state of fermentation:</i>] AZ mentions it as applied to him who is low, abject, or contemptible; who is held to be weak: and he says that ظَلَمْتُ السِّقَا=ءَ means “ I gave [the milk of] the skin to be drunk before it had attained to maturity [so as to be fit for the process of churning]: ” (T:) or مُرَوَّبٌ signifies <i>not churned, but having in it its ferment;</i> and the prov. is applied to him who is constrained to do something that is difficult, and to become in a state of abasement, or ignominy, and does not manifest any disapproval.
