                   A man <i>smitten,</i> or <i>blown upon, by the</i> [<i>north,</i> or <i>northerly,</i>] <i>wind called</i> <br>\
                  شَمَال: (S, O:) and in like manner, a meadow, and a pool of water left by a torrent; (O;) or, applied to this last, <i>smitten by the wind thus called so as to become cool:</i> (S:) and hence, with ة, wine (tropical:)  <i>cool to the taste;</i> (S, O, TA; *) or wine <i>exposed to the</i> <br>\
                  شَمَال <br>\
                  <i>and so rendered cool and pleasant:</i> (TA: see also شَمُولٌ:) and fire <i>upon which the wind called the</i> <br>\
                  شَمَال <br>\
                  <i>has blown:</i> (S, O:) and a night <i>cold, with</i> [<i>wind that is called</i>] شَمَال. (TA.) ―         -b2-  [Hence,] (tropical:)  One <i>whose natural dispositions are liked, approved,</i> or <i>found pleasant:</i> (K:) from [the same epithet applied to] water upon which the شَمَال has blown, and which it has cooled: or, as ISd thinks, from شَمُولٌ [q. v.]: (TA:) or مَشْمُولُ الخَلَايِقِ a man <i>whose natural dispositions are commended;</i> as being likened to wine that is commended: and also <i>whose natural dispositions are discommended;</i> as though from الشَّمَالُ, because they do not commend it when it disperses the clouds: (Har p. 285:) [for] اَخْلَاقٌ مَشْمُولَةٌ [sometimes] means <i>discommended, evil, natural dispositions.</i> (IAar, ISk, TA.)
