                  لَا بُدَّ and لَامَحَالَةٌ [<i>There is no avoiding it; it is absolutely necessary;</i> &amp;amp;c.]: then, by reason of frequency of usage, employed in the manner of an oath, as meaning حَقًّا [<i>verily,</i> or <i>truly</i>]; wherefore, as in the case of an oath, ل is prefixed to its complement, (Fr, S, Msb, K, *) so that they say, لَا جَرَمَ لَا=تِيَنّكَ [<i>Verily I will come to thee</i>], (Fr, S, K,) and لَا جَرَمَ لَاَفْعَلَنَّ كَذَا [<i>Verily I will do thus</i>], (S, Msb, *) and لَا جَرَمَ لَقَدْ كَانَ كَذَا and لَا ذَا جَرَمَ and لَا ذَا جَرَ [<i>Verily it was thus,</i> or <i>verily such a thing happened</i>]: (IAar, TA:) ISd says, Kh asserts that جَرَمَ [or لَا جَرَمَ] is only a reply to something said before it; as when a man says, “ They did such a thing, ” and you say, لَا جَرَمَ اَنَّهُمْ سَيَنْدَمُونَ, or اَنَّهُ سَيَكُونُ كَذَا <br>\
